Visualizing Pharmecuetical Data

This project consolidated and analyzed data from a study testing different drugs on mice to stop the growth and spread of cancer. This study measured things like survival rate, tumor size, and metastasis. Each mouse group was treated with one of eight drugs for up to 50 days or until they died. The goal of this study was to determine which drug held the most potential for use in cancer therapy for humans. In the end, the drugs Capomulin and Ramicane had the most positive results for reducing tumor size and extending the survival of the mice.
